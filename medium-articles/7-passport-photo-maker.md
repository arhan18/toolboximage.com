## Why You Need a Passport Photo Maker

### 1. Government Photo Requirements Are Strict
Every country has specific requirements:
- **US Passport:** 2×2 inches (600×600 px), white background, head 50–69% of frame
- **UK Passport:** 35×45 mm (413×531 px), light gray background, head 29–34 mm
- **India Passport:** 35×45 mm (413×531 px), pure white background, no glasses
- **Canada Passport:** 50×70 mm (591×827 px), white background, ears must be visible
- **China Passport:** 33×48 mm (390×567 px), pure white background
- **EU/Schengen:** 35×45 mm (413×531 px), white or light gray background

Getting these wrong is the #1 reason passport applications are delayed.

### 2. Photo Studio Costs Add Up
A single passport photo costs $10–15 in most countries. For a family of four needing passport + visa photos, that's $80–120. Doing it yourself costs nothing.

### 3. Digital Submission Is Common Now
Most passport renewals and visa applications accept digital photo uploads. You don't need a physical print — just a correctly sized digital file.

## How to Take a Good Passport Photo at Home

### Do's
- Stand against a plain white or light-colored wall
- Face the camera directly with both eyes visible
- Use even, diffused lighting (window light from the front is ideal)
- Keep a neutral expression
- Wear everyday clothing (no uniforms or white shirts that blend with the background)

### Don'ts
- No shadows on your face or background
- No sunglasses or tinted glasses
- No hats or head coverings (except for religious reasons)
- No filters, editing, or retouching
- Don't use a selfie — have someone else take the photo or use a timer
