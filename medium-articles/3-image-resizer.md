## Why Resize Images?

### 1. Social Media Platform Requirements
Each platform has specific optimal dimensions:
- **Instagram Square:** 1080×1080 px
- **Instagram Portrait:** 1080×1350 px
- **Instagram Story:** 1080×1920 px
- **Facebook Post:** 1200×630 px
- **X/Twitter Post:** 1200×675 px
- **LinkedIn Banner:** 1584×396 px
- **YouTube Thumbnail:** 1280×720 px

### 2. Email Attachments
Most email providers block or reject attachments over 25 MB. Resizing high-resolution photos to 1024×768 px typically brings them under 500 KB — safe for any email service.

### 3. Website Performance
Serving a 4000×3000 px photo when your website displays it at 800×600 is wasteful. Resizing images to their display size before uploading can cut page weight by 80%.

### 4. Print Specifications
Need a 300 DPI image for printing? Calculate the pixel dimensions (width in inches × 300) and resize accordingly.

## Understanding Resize Modes

| Mode | What It Does | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Fit** | Scales image to fit within target dimensions. Adds padding if aspect ratios don't match. | Product photos, email attachments, preserving full image |
| **Fill** | Scales to fill target dimensions. Crops excess. | Social media posts, cover images, thumbnails |
| **Stretch** | Forces the image to exactly match target dimensions regardless of aspect ratio. | When exact pixel dimensions are non-negotiable and slight distortion is acceptable |

## Step-Down Resizing for Maximum Quality

Resizing a very large image (e.g., 6000×4000 px → 800×600 px) in a single step can introduce artifacts. The ToolBox Image Resizer uses **step-down resizing** — it halves the dimensions repeatedly until it's close to the target, then does the final resize. This preserves significantly more detail than single-step resizing.
