## Why Watermark Your Images?

### 1. Brand Recognition
Every time someone shares your watermarked image, your brand travels with it. A subtle logo or website URL in the corner is free advertising.

### 2. Deter Theft
While a determined thief can crop or clone-stamp a watermark, most casual image theft is opportunistic. A visible watermark discourages 95% of unauthorized use.

### 3. Client Proofing
When sharing draft images with clients, watermarks prevent premature use while they review and provide feedback.

### 4. SEO Credit
If your watermark includes your website URL, every share of your image becomes a potential backlink.

## Watermark Best Practices

**Subtle > Obtrusive.** A watermark that ruins your image is worse than no watermark. The goal is protection, not punishment.

- **Opacity:** 30–50% is usually sufficient
- **Position:** Bottom-right corner is standard, but test other positions
- **Size:** 2–5% of the image dimensions is a good starting point
- **Font:** Clean, simple, sans-serif (avoid decorative fonts)
- **Color:** White with partial transparency works on most images (add a shadow for dark areas)

## When to Use Tiled Watermarks

Tiled watermarks (repeating across the full image) are ideal for:

- **Stock photography previews** — prevents unauthorized use while showing composition
- **Client proofs** — makes it impossible to crop out the watermark
- **Portfolio images** — protect your work on public portfolio sites

For social media and marketing images, a single watermark in the corner is usually sufficient.

## Watermark Positioning Guide

| Position | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| **Bottom Right** | General purpose, follows natural eye movement |
| **Bottom Left** | Photos with busy lower-right areas |
| **Top Left** | Dark images (text is more visible against light sky) |
| **Center** | Maximum visibility, stock photo proofs |
| **Tile** | Maximum protection, preview images |
