## Why Do Image Formats Matter?

Different platforms and applications require different image formats:

- **JPEG (.jpg)** — Universally supported. Best for photos. Doesn't support transparency.
- **PNG (.png)** — Supports transparency. Best for logos, screenshots, and graphics with text.
- **WebP (.webp)** — Google's modern format. 25–35% smaller than JPEG at the same quality. Supported in all major browsers since 2024.
- **AVIF (.avif)** — The newest format. Up to 50% smaller than JPEG. Better compression than WebP, but slightly less browser support.
- **GIF (.gif)** — Still the king of simple animations. Limited to 256 colors.
- **SVG (.svg)** — Vector format. Infinitely scalable. Best for icons and illustrations.

---

## When to Convert Between Formats

### HEIC/HEIF → JPEG
iPhone photos are stored in HEIC format by default. These won't open in many online tools, Windows photo viewers, or older software.

### PNG → JPEG
Need to submit a photo to a website that only accepts JPEG? PNG files are larger, so converting to JPEG also saves bandwidth.

### JPEG → WebP
If you manage a website, converting all images to WebP can cut page load times by 25–35%. Major sites like YouTube, Netflix, and Facebook already use WebP.

### WebP → PNG
Need transparency? WebP supports it, but not all design tools do. Convert to PNG for maximum compatibility.

### JPEG → PNG
Adding text overlays or transparent elements to a photo requires PNG. Convert your JPEG base to PNG first, then edit.
